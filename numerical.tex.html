\noindent \textit{Investor }$X$\textit{ and investor }$Y$\textit{ simultaneously decide how much to invest in a new project. Payoffs are increasing in how much the other investor invests. To be precise the payoffs to }$X$\textit{ and }$Y$\textit{ are:} 
 
\noindent $U^x(x,y)\ =\ x(\textrm{½}y\ +\ 75\ --\ x)U^y(x,y)\ =\ y(\textrm{½}x\ +\ 75\ --\ y)$\textit{Where }$x$\textit{ and }$y$\textit{ denote the investment level of }$X$\textit{ and }$Y$\textit{, respectively. For both }$X$\textit{ and }$Y$\textit{, the minimum investment level is}$\ 0$\textit{ and the maximum level is }$200$\textit{.} 
 
\noindent \textit{} 
 
\begin{enumerate} 
\item \textit{ What are the Nash equilibrium investment levels?} 
\end{enumerate} 
 
\noindent \textit{} 
 
\noindent In order to find the Nash equilibrium investment levels we take the first order derivative of both utility functions. Then we set this equal to $0$ and solve this for $x$ and $y$ respectively. We substitute the expression of $y$ into the function of $x$ to determine the equilibrium. 
\[\frac{dU^x}{dx}=\frac{1}{2}y+75-2x=0\]  
\[2x=\frac{1}{2}y+75\]  
\[x=\frac{1}{4}y+37.5\]  
\[\frac{dU^y}{dy}=\frac{1}{2}x+75-2y=0\]  
\[2y=\frac{1}{2}x+75\]  
\[y=\frac{1}{4}x+37.5\]  
\[x=\frac{1}{4}\left(\frac{1}{4}x+37.5\right)+37.5\]  
\[x=\frac{1}{16}x+9.375+37.5\]  
\[\frac{15}{16}x=46.875\]  
\[x=50\to y=\frac{1}{4}*50+37.5=50\]  
So the Nash equilibrium investment levels are equal to $50$ for both investors $(50,50)$. 
 
\noindent  
 
\begin{enumerate} 
\item  \textit{Suppose investor }$X$\textit{ is type level-1. How much will investor }$X$\textit{ invest?} 
\end{enumerate} 
 
\noindent \textit{} 
 
\noindent Since investor $X$ is a type level-1 player, he assumes player $Y$ to be a type level-0 player which chooses random. Since player $Y$ chooses random from the interval of $[0,200]$, we assume player $Y$ to choose $100$.  
 
\noindent Substituting this value for $y$ is the best-response function of player $X$ gives: 
\[x=\frac{1}{4}*100+37.5=62.5\]  
So player $X$ will invest $62.5$. 
 
\noindent  
 
\begin{enumerate} 
\item  \textit{Suppose investor }$X$\textit{ is type C-2 (believing that investor }$Y$\textit{ is a mixture of level-0 and level-1, with proportions determined by }$=1.5$\textit{). How much will investor }$X$\textit{ invest?} 
\end{enumerate} 
 
\noindent \textit{} 
 
\noindent First, we need to determine the proportions of level-0 and level-1 players (actually player $Y$ is a mixture of the two types). We do this by using a Poisson distribution and taking $=1.5$. 
\[f\left(0\right)=\frac{e^{-1.5}{1.5}^0}{0!}=0.223\]  
\[f\left(1\right)=\frac{e^{-1.5}{1.5}^1}{1!}=0.335\]  
 
\[\frac{0.223*100+0.335*62.5}{0.223+0.335}=77.487\]  
 
\[x=\frac{1}{4}*77.487+37.5=56.872\]  
So player $X$ will invest $56.872$.
